Research Finding 1 of 4 · Longitudinal Sentiment Analysis · 2021–2025

The Drift

From 2021 to 2025, negative sentiment in Canadian primary care Reddit discourse climbed from 37.4% to an annual peak of 41.1%. This is the trajectory — month by month — and what it tells us about a system under sustained, growing stress.

Key Findings

Six numbers that define the drift

2021 baseline
37.4%

Average annual negative sentiment in 2021 — already high before the crisis entered public consciousness. The floor, before it started rising.

2024 annual peak
41.1%

The worst annual average in the dataset. A +3.7 percentage-point rise from 2021 that compresses four years of policy inaction into a single number.

Single worst months

Year-by-Year Analysis

A slow-motion story of a system under pressure

2021

The quiet beginning

7,596 records, 633 per month average. 37.4% negativity — already elevated, but the floor is visible. February 2021 is the single best month in five years: 25.0% negative. By April, that same year spikes to 44.2%, a warning that most ignored.

37.4% avg neg · 7,596 records · Best: Feb 25.0% · Worst: Apr 44.2%
2022

The rupture year

Everything changes in 2022. Volume nearly doubles to 951 records per month. Negativity hits 40.4%. July 2022 produces 1,577 records — the first major volume spike. November 2022 reaches 44.3%, a new ceiling. The conversation is no longer fringe.

40.4% avg neg · 11,415 records · Volume peak: Jul 1,577 · Sentiment peak: Nov 44.3%
2023

Resignation becomes the baseline

At 38.8% average, 2023 sits between 2021 and 2022 — but the character has changed. Where 2022 posts were shocked and angry, 2023 posts are tired. The monthly range barely varies. Chronic frustration has replaced acute crisis as the dominant tone. December 2023 reaches 44.1%.

38.8% avg neg · 12,309 records · Steadiest year — 9.7pp spread · Dec 44.1% closes the year dark
2024

The peak — and no one notices

2024 is the worst year by every measure. 41.1% annual average. 1,446 records per month, with March reaching 2,284. The spring peak is extraordinary: April and June both hit 44.6%. What is most striking is the absence of any corresponding policy announcement that the data can detect. The system breaks a record and the record is not acknowledged.

41.1% avg neg · 17,358 records · March 2024: 2,284 records · April & June 2024: 44.6% — the highest in 55 months
2025

A cautious dip

January–August 2025 shows the first sustained stretch of lower negativity since mid-2021. April (32.6%) and May (32.1%) fall to three-year lows. At 36.8% partial-year average, there is a plausible case for stabilisation. But the volume remains high (1,085/month) and alternating high/low months make a definitive conclusion impossible. The drift may have paused. It has not reversed.

36.8% avg neg (Jan–Aug) · April 32.6% & May 32.1% — lowest since 2021 · n = 8,682 records

Interpretation

What the drift tells a health policy researcher

The most important feature of this dataset is not any individual peak — it is the floor. In 2021, the best month (February) saw 25.0% negative sentiment. By 2024, the best month (December) was 36.2%. The floor has risen 11.2 percentage points in four years. This is not a crisis that spikes and recovers; it is a system whose resting state of frustration is continuously worsening.

The volume surge is equally significant. In 2021, 633 Canadians per month were posting about primary care. By 2024, that had grown to 1,446 per month — not because more people use Reddit, but because more people have something urgent to say. High-volume negative months are self-reinforcing: frustrated posts attract replies, which attract more posts, which makes the problem more visible, which frustrates more people.

What 2025 Cannot Yet Tell Us

The cautious case for stabilisation

April and May 2025 are the lowest-negativity months since 2021. There are three possible explanations: genuine systemic improvement (new doctors entering practice, NP scope expansions), a seasonal effect that will reverse in autumn, or simply noise in data that has not yet been fully captured for recent months.

The data cannot distinguish between these scenarios. What it can say is this: as of August 2025, the conversation is still 3.4 percentage points more negative than January 2021 — the very first month we measured. The drift has paused. Whether that pause becomes a reversal will be visible in the autumn 2025 data.

The policy implication is direct: if provincial health ministries are tracking Reddit sentiment as a real-time signal, the spring 2025 dip should not be read as success. It should be read as a question mark — one that October and November 2025 will answer.

The question is not whether the system is struggling. The data answered that in January 2021.

Every year in this dataset has a worse annual average than 2021 — until 2025, which is trending better but remains above any 2021 month except February and July. The floor has risen 11 points. The question is whether the policies launched in 2022, 2023, and 2024 are actually reaching Canadians — and whether they can see it in the months that remain to be measured.

Method. Sentiment classification using a four-label VADER ML ensemble (Positive / Negative / Neutral / Mixed) applied to 89,398 Reddit records from primary care subreddits, January 2021 – August 2025. Negative sentiment percentage = neg / (pos + neg + neu + mix) per month. September 2022 is interpolated from adjacent months. Annual averages weight all months equally; 2025 is partial (January–August).

Source. Reddit Canadian primary care subreddits · VADER ensemble · n = 89,398 · Memorial University, Faculty of Medicine · 2021–2025.
