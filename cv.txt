RESEARCH Introduction
My research is centered on building equitable, responsive, and community-engaged health systems, with a focus on primary health care, community health interventions, and health policy. I approach these topics through a health systems and policy lens, applying mixed methods to understand and improve access, delivery, and equity in care both in Canada and globally.
In Canada, I am a co-investigator on a CIHR-funded implementation science project evaluating a complex, team-based primary care intervention in Newfoundland and Labrador. This work aims to enhance interdisciplinary collaboration and improve service delivery in rural and remote settings. Complementing this, I co-lead a project investigating refugee health care access using Functional Resonance Analysis Methodology (FRAM) to identify systemic barriers and inform adaptive models of culturally safe care. Previously, I led a national environmental scan and scoping review of patient-centered models in Canada to identify best practices and policy gaps. I was also a principle applicant for a CIHR project to assess health impacts of cannabis policies in Newfoundland and Labrador. Following that project, I have collaborated on multiple studies assessing youth decision-making, cannabis health literacy, and public health communication strategies. I co-led a project funded by the Canadian Foundation for Healthcare Improvement (CFHI) focused on enhancing the lives of older Canadians in long-term care settings in Newfoundland and Labrador. This initiative aimed to identify and implement resident-centered strategies to improve quality of care and well-being for aging populations.
Globally, my research on the Afghan Community Health Worker (CHW) program—conducted during my doctoral studies—has generated new insights into the scalability, gender dynamics, and sustainability of community-based primary care in fragile health systems. This work contributed to the development of WHO’s guidelines on CHW programs. At the same time, as part of a regional project on the implementation of health-related Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) in South Asia, I led the Afghanistan country study and contributed to comparative analyses across seven countries. This work, funded by IDRC, assessed national readiness, policy integration, and accountability mechanisms, and informed regional policy dialogues on SDG progress. I co-led a CIHR-funded study on the social determinants of multidrug-resistant tuberculosis (MDR-TB) in Sikkim, India. This pilot project used a health systems and SDG lens to explore how structural factors such as poverty, housing, and health service access contribute to the persistence of MDR-TB, aiming to inform integrated, equity-oriented responses.
My ongoing research program continues to focus on primary care as a foundation for equitable and effective health systems. Current projects include evaluating team-based primary care models, exploring interprofessional collaboration within these teams, understanding shortage of family physicians in NL through a stock and flow method, and assessing the implementation of Family Care Teams in Newfoundland and Labrador. Internationally, my graduate students examine how primary care can drive health system strengthening in low-resources settings including Bangladesh and Afghanistan.

SERVICE and Academic Leadership
I am an Afghan-Canadian physician-researcher and Associate Professor in Memorial University’s Faculty of Medicine. My academic journey—an M.D. from Balkh University followed by an M.A. in Health Communication and a Ph.D. in Population Health at the University of Ottawa—anchors a career devoted to building equitable, community-engaged health systems . As Interim Assistant Dean for Social Accountability, and previously Faculty Lead for Global Health & Health Equity and MPH Program Coordinator, I steer faculty-wide strategy, curriculum renewal, and quality assurance to embed equity, cultural safety, and global perspectives across teaching, research, and service .
My research program has attracted more than $3.8 million in competitive supporting projects on team-based primary care, refugee health, youth mental-health services, and cannabis policy. Findings from this work have informed World Health Organization guidance on Community Health Worker programs and shaped Canadian health workforce reforms. I have authored and co-authored 28 peer-reviewed publications, alongside policy briefs, book chapters, and commentaries . A committed educator, I have developed graduate courses in health policy, equity, and knowledge translation, supervised more than fifty MPH practica and a growing roster of master’s and doctoral students, and regularly deliver guest lectures and grand rounds.
Guided by a leadership philosophy that blends evidence, partnership, and advocacy, I work to advance social accountability and improve population health locally in Newfoundland & Labrador and globally in low-resource settings.
